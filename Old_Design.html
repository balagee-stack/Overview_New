Knowledge Path

Throughout Grade 1, you'll teach eight four-week units, all aligned to grade-level science, social studies, and ELA standards.

Students will build on the knowledge they gained in Grade K as they learn about new grade-level topics across the same eight knowledge strands.

Grade K

Living Things Have Needs

Understanding that all living things have basic needs for survival supports students as they explore the parts and behaviors living things utilize to meet those needs.
Grade 1

Living Things Grow and Survive

Examining how living things use parts and behaviors to survive and care for their young reveals patterns of interaction. This prepares students to study how plants and animals depend on one another within habitats.
Grade 2

Living Things and Their Habitats

Observing how living things rely on one another for survival shows that each species has features that help it meet its needs. This sets up learning how traits enable living things to survive and thrive.
Grade 3

Living Things and Their Traits

Understanding that living things have inherited behavioral and physical traits supports students as they learn how organisms use their senses to guide behavior and stay alive.
Grade 4

Living Things and Their Senses

Essential Question
Learning how organisms use their senses to survive and interact shows that these behaviors influence ecosystems. This prepares students to explore how energy moves through organisms within a food web.
Grade 5

Living Things and Their Interactions

Essential Question:

How do living things use their senses to interact with the world?

Essential Question

×

In this unit, your students will build on their knowledge from prior grade levels to focus on how living things use their senses, including sight, sound/hearing, touch, taste, and smell, to gather information, send and receive signals, guide their actions, and learn from other living and non-living things in their ecosystems.

How do living things use their senses
to interact with the world?

Knowledge Goal 1
Living things take in information from their environment using certain senses, such as smell, sight, hearing, taste, and touch.
Knowledge Goal 2
Living things, including plants and animals, rely on senses to receive information, send signals, and react to the world around them.
Knowledge Goal 3
Living things, including humans, can use their senses to gain new information to guide their actions and learn new things.
Enduring Understanding
Living things use their senses to interpret, interact with, and learn from the world around them in order to survive.

Week 1 Knowledge Goal

Living things take in information from their environment using certain senses, such as smell, sight, hearing, taste, and touch.

  • Students read a chapter from the award-winning Pax: Journey Home, to learn how animals receive different types of information through their senses, process the information in their brain, and respond to the information in different ways.
  • Students gain the understanding that animals have body parts that capture and convey different kinds of information needed for growth and survival, for example, eyes for light, ears for sounds, and nose for smell.
  • Students gain knowledge about how sense receptors are specialized for particular kinds of information, which may then be processed by an animal’s brain, with some information stored as memories.
  • Students begin to explore how animals are able to use their perceptions and memories to guide their actions.

Week 2 Knowledge Goal

Living things, including plants and animals, rely on senses to receive information, send signals, and react to the world around them.

  • Students read an article about “Tricky Trees and Gifted Giraffes” to learn how plants and animals send and receive different types of information that is perceived by animals senses and respond to the information in different ways.
  • Students learn how some responses to information are instinctive, that is, animals’ brains are organized so that they do not have to think about how to respond to certain stimuli.
  • Students learn how, like animals, plants also have different parts that help them survive, grow, produce more plants, and sense and react to stimuli.
  • Students gain knowledge about how animals respond to these inputs with behaviors that help them survive. Plants also respond to some external inputs.

Week 3 Knowledge Goal

Living things, including humans, can use their senses to gain new information to guide their actions and learn new things.

  • Students continue to explore how different sense receptors are specialized for particular kinds of information, which may then be processed by the brain, with some information stored as memories.
  • Students gain understanding about how humans are able to use their perceptions and memories to guide their actions.
  • Students gain knowledge about how some responses to information are instinctive, that is, our brains are organized so that they do not have to think about how to respond to certain stimuli and other responses are intentional.

Week 4 Unit Enduring Understanding

Living things use their senses to interpret, interact with, and learn from the world around them in order to survive.

  • Students continue to explore how animals use their perceptions and memories to guide their actions by reading a short narrative told from the perspective of a bird, called a masked weaver.
  • Students continue to build knowledge about how animals have both instinctive and learned responses by exploring the process of how a bird learns to build its nest.

Culminating Tasks

Culminating Task Writing

Text Type: Informative/Explanatory

Source: “Tornado on the Horizon”

Scoring: Use the Writing portion of the Culminating Task Rubric to score students’ essays and collect baseline data to inform instruction in future units.

Prompt

Write an essay that identifies and explains two tools meteorologists use to predict tornadoes. Be sure to use facts, details, and other information from "Tornado on the Horizon" in your explanation. You will share your writing with your teacher and classmates.

Culminating Task Project

Task: Book Trailers that Spark Interest

Task: Students demonstrate their knowledge by creating and presenting a poster that shows how to prepare for an emergency weather event. During presentations, students speak clearly, at a good pace, and in complete sentences to explain their Emergency Preparedness Kit Posters.
