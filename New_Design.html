Knowledge Journey
Topic
Throughout Grade 1, you'll teach eight four-week units, all aligned to grade-level science, social studies, and ELA standards.
Students will build on the knowledge they gained in Grade K as they learn about new grade-level topics across the same eight knowledge strands. ​
ConceptuallyCoherent Texts
All units in Grade 1 are centered on high-quality, conceptually coherently text sets that provide regular opportunities for students to engage in a rich range and volume of reading. There’s a 50/50 balance of literary and informational texts, and selections include both short and extended works. ​
Texts for Grade 1 are organized into three categories based on their instructional purpose. Across categories, these texts support the unit’s knowledge-building goals, and collectively advance students’ development of grade-level ELA expectations
Complex Knowledge-Building
Multimedia videos and full-length books that teachers read aloud during daily Read to Build Knowledge lessons and Write to Build Understanding lessons.
Learn More
Decodable Text
Texts students read during daily Foundational Literacy lessons, WINN lessons, and independent reading time. Read during daily Foundational Literacy lessons, WINN lessons, and independent reading time.
Learn More
Transition Text
Texts designed to support students’ transition from highly decodable materials toward broader, more varied reading experiences. These texts may be read, as appropriate, during WINN Time or other independent reading opportunities.

Collaborative Core Lessons
In Grade 1, students engage in 90-minutes of daily Collaborative Core whole-group Lessons. The core lessonsrequire active engagement where students participate meaningfully and take ownership of their learning through embedded instructional routines and discussion protocols. There are threelesson types in Collaborative Core:​
  • Foundational Literacy
  • Read to Build Knowledge
  • Write to Build Understanding
One layer is lesson structure, one layer is integrated support.

WINN Time Lessons and Tasks
WINN Time (What I Need Now) is a flexible instructional block designed to provide targeted support and enrichment opportunities for all students. Whether scheduled within or outside your district's literacy block, WINN Time enables you to address specific student needs through data-driven differentiated instruction. WINN Time is made up a targeted small-group lessons and independent practice activities. The chart below highlights the three types of lessons.
MAX Literacy WINN Lessons
Lessons Location/Description
WINN Foundational Literacy Lessons
  • Provided each week in the Unit Teacher’s Edition.
  • Short Phonological Awareness, Decoding, Encoding, Fluency, Vocabulary, and Comprehension lessons offered at three levels of support: Reteach, Maintain, and Extend.
WINN Read to Build Knowledge Lessons
  • Provided each week in the Unit Teacher’s Edition.
  • Four lessons per week that reteach Vocabulary, Comprehension, and Grammar learning outcomes introduced during the Collaborative Core Read to Build Knowledge lessons.
WINNSupport and AccelerationLesson Bank
  • Provided in the digital Plan and Teach library.
  • A collection of explicit intervention lessons designed to address student needs identified through assessment data and recommendations. These lessons support on-grade and below-grade learning outcomes across Phonological Awareness, Phonics, Fluency, Print Concepts, Comprehension, and Vocabulary/Morphology. Each lesson includes an exit-ticket quick check and recommended next steps.
The Grade 1 Independent Practice activities include:
Task Location/Description
Adaptive Digital Practice 		Each week, digital games (accessed on the student dashboard) reinforce the Decoding, Encoding, Vocabulary, and Grammar skills taught during Collaborative Core lessons. Students first play an on-level game and then, based on their performance, play a second game at an appropriate level of support (on, above, or below level). Instant data is provided to inform reteaching. The weekly WINN Time Planner suggests when students should complete each game, aligned to the timing of instruction.
Purposeful Play Centers (K–1 only) 		Instructions and materials for two hands-on play centers per unit are provided in each Unit Teacher Planner. A Literacy Center provides creative activities that develop creativity, decoding, encoding, fine-motor, and social skills. A Knowledge-Building Center
Tasks from Collaborative Core Lessons 		Paper-and-pencil practice activities that reinforce core lessons are included in the MyMAX Book, Handwriting to the MAX, and in practice reproducibles that extend core instruction. The weekly WINN Time Planner suggests specific activities for each day of the week, aligned to the timing of instruction.
Independent Reading to Build Fluency and Knowledge (K–1) 		A wide variety of independent reading titles related to the unit topic are provided to support students to engage in a rich range and volume of reading, including both decodable and authentic options. The MAXLiteracy Independent Reading Handbook provides detailed information to support your independent reading program.

5. Assessment Opportunities
Types of Assessments
Screening, Diagnostic, and Monitoring Assessments
In Grade 1, teachers use Screening, Diagnostic, and Progress Monitoring Assessments to evaluate student skills and providemeaningful feedback to teachers. Screening tools are used to assess all students at the beginning of the school year to get a baseline measure of skill mastery and, critically, to identify students who shows signs of being at-risk for reading difficulties.
Assessment Type Purpose Assessment Tools SuggestedFrequency Results and Next Steps

  • Assess 360 (G3-5)
 3x per year If students are identified as at-risk:
  • Administer RAN task
  • Administer Diagnostic Assessments based on student subtest performance
Diagnostic Identify specific skill areas for additional instruction and intervention
  • Comprehensive Phonics and Spelling Surveys
